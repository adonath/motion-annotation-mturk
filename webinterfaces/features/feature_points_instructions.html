Feature Point Annotation - Instructions

What to Do

The aim is to find eight noticeable points in the first image and their corresponding locations in the second image as accurately as possible.

Interface Instructions

The first step is:
  • Remember to accept the hit before you proceed!
  • Please find a "good" point (see below) by moving the mouse pointer on the left image. Begin with the best point you can find.
  • When the red circle is exactly above the "good" point, left-click once.
  • Good points are features you will easily find in the second image, such as sharp corners or the centers of small spots.
  • Please be sure that the selected point is located such that you can easily relocalize it with high accuracy.
  • If you accidentially clicked: no worries, you can later delete wrong points using the waste icon in the table on the left.
  • Once you've selected the point, the region around (marked by the red rectangle) will appear in the magnifier below.
The second step is:
  • Try to position the cursor in the right image onto exactly the same point as in the first image.
  • Use the magnifier in the middle to work as accurate as possible.
  • The third magnifier on the right shows the difference between the to regions: If you are close to the first point, it will get darker. If you are on the right point it will be nearly black.
  • Another help for finding the right position in the second image, is the number shown in the red box: It should be as small as possible.
  • Once you are satisfied: Click again and proceed to step three.
The third step is:
  • Fine-tune the position of the point in the second image, by using the a-, s-, d-, w-key.
  • As you tap the keys, you will notice that the cursor moves only minimal and that the error in the red box changes.
  • Try to find the minimum of the number in the red box, but don't move away too much from your original position.
  • Once you've found the position, where the error is as small as possible, hit the ENTER key.
  • The point will now be listed in the table on the left. You can remove points, by clicking on the waste icon in the table.
  • Continue with step one, until you have found EIGHT corresponding points!
General hints:
  • The quality (i.e. the agreement of the two points) is very important!
  • The points you choose should be one the one hand be evenly distributed in the left image and on the other hand be as easily trackable as possible.
  • If in doubt which point to choose, the "trackability" is more important.
  • Once all points have been added, press the submit button at the top of the page to submit your results.

Further Hints and Troubleshooting

  • In case you do this task for the first time and want to practice: You will always find a few HITs in the Mechanical Turk Sandbox. Search for "Image Segmentation".
  • Please take our examples as a lower accuracy limit of your work!
  • For some images you can change the contrast, if the image is too dark.
  • Unfortunately some images might not be available in colors.
  • If the image is not displayed or to big, please reload the page.
  • Currently this is not working with Internet Explorer. Please try Mozilla Firefox.

Why Solve This HIT?

We are trying to find pixel positions in image sequences (videos) which can be found again in subsequent frames (time steps).
This is called feature tracking or correspondence search. Scientists have been trying to solve this problem automatically for decades, but it seems very difficult for the computer to solve this task.
Yet, researchers have found some ways to do feature tracking and they would like to know if they can outsmart us humans.
Therefore, we now ask you to outsmart computers by finding the best possible features in a first image and their respective correspondences in a second image.
Once we know such correspondences we can do a lot of fun things! Have a look here, here and here to get a rough idea ;)
